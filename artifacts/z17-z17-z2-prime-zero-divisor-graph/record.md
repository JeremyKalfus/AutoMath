## approach_A

Structural / invariant approach.

- The singleton `C` is the hinge vertex: it is adjacent to every vertex in `A ∪ B ∪ D`, and every vertex of `D` is adjacent only to `C`.
- So the clean family move is again to assign label `1` to `C`. Then all edges `A-C`, `B-C`, and `C-D` are automatically valid, and the whole `D` class becomes unconstrained.
- After fixing `C = 1`, the only nontrivial arithmetic interfaces are:
  - `A-B`
  - `A-F`
  - `B-E`
- This suggests a reusable sufficient condition: choose a finite prime-support set `S`, place all labels on `A ∪ E` inside the multiplicative semigroup generated by `S`, and place all labels on `B ∪ F` among numbers coprime to every prime in `S`.
- Then every edge across `A-B`, `A-F`, and `B-E` is automatically coprime, while `D` can absorb the residual labels.

Brief self-check:

- The hinge reduction is exact, not heuristic: once `C = 1`, there is no remaining arithmetic constraint touching `D`.
- The graph problem is now an interval-partition problem on `64` constrained labels rather than a `321`-vertex search.
