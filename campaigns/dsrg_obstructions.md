## Why It Exists

The ledger contains many nontrivial dsrg runs with reusable exact structure:

- fixed-vertex partitions into `M/O/I/N`
- exact three-cell and five-cell quotients
- forced spectra and rank identities
- no-abelian-Cayley or no-normal subtheorems
- local graph and triangle-count packages

These are better than random failed runs, but most still stop short of a referee-ready theorem.
